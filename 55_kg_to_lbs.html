First of all, you should know a little about the history of kilograms and pounds

A kilogram (symbol: kg) is the International System of Units' fundamental unit of mass (SI). Currently, its definition is predicated on the Planck constant's fixed numerical value, h. The word kilogram is derived from the French "kilogramme," which was created by prefixing the Late Latin word "gramma," which meant "a tiny weight," with Greek terminology that meant "a thousand."

A pound (symbol: lb) is a mass unit that is utilized by the imperial and US customary measuring systems. The precise weight of the international avoirdupois pound, or today's standard pound, is 0.45359237 kilos. 16 avoirdupois ounces make up an avoirdupois pound.
