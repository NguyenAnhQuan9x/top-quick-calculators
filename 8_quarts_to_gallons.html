First of all, you should know a little about the history of quarts and gallons

The quart (symbol: qt) is a volumetric unit used in both the imperial and metric measurement systems in the United States. There are various quart definitions. A liquid quart in the US is roughly equivalent to 0.946353 liters, while a dry quart is roughly equivalent to 1.101221 liters. The imperial quart is equivalent to 1.136523 in the UK. The quart is a quarter of a gallon in both the US and the UK.

A pound (symbol: lb) is a volume measurement unit used in both the imperial and US customary systems of measurement that is specifically related to liquid capacity. 231 cubic inches are regarded as one US gallon (3.785 liters). The imperial gallon, on the other hand, is defined as 4.54609 liters and is used in the UK, Canada, and several Caribbean countries. Gallons are frequently used in the US to refer to larger containers like one-gallon milk cartons and half-gallon ice cream tubs. Additionally, gallons are frequently employed to express fuel economy in the US and certain of its territories.
