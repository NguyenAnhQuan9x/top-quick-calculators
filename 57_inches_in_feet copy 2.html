First of all, you should know a little about the history of inches and feet

An inches is a unit of measurement that adheres to the American Standard. One foot is equivalent to twelve inches. A further division into smaller units follows the inch. One inch is divided into 16 units. One-sixteenth of an inch (1/16 inch) is the size of each unit. These 16 tiny units are combined into a larger unit called an inch, which is made up of 2/16ths (which is mathematically equivalent to an eighth). These two 1/8th units are 1/4 inch wide (also commonly called a quarter of an inch). Again, two of these quarter-inch divisions equal one inch (two quarters are mathematically reduced to one inch). Next, three units of 1/4 inch (often referred to as "three quarters of an inch") equal 3/8. Finally, 1 inch is equivalent to 4 1/4th units.

A foot (pl.feet) is a standard length unit used in both the present US customary unit system and the Imperial system. 0.3048 meters are equal to one foot. Since the Roman and Greek eras, this length measurement has been used in Europe. A yard is made up of three feet and 12 inches.
